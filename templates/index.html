Plots


Poincaré

The Poincaré plot is a scatter graph that visualizes the correlation between two consecutive data points in a time-series (i.e., x-axis: An versus y-axis: An+1). It has extensively been used in the analysis heart rate variability and has enabled researchers to measure short- and long-term variability separately.


Lomb-Scargle Periodogram

Lomb-Scargle Periodogram is a method of estimating a frequency spectrum, based on a least squares fit of sinusoids to data samples. When analysing the HRV, the Lomb-Scargle method is prefered over the Welch Periodogram because the data is varied and not evenly spaced.


Detrended Fluctuation Analysis

Detrended Fluctuation Analysis has proven useful in revealing the extent of long-range correlations in time series. Furthermore, it is suitable for the analysis of nonstationary time series and has confirmed the existence of persistent long-range correlations in healthy heart rate variability data.
